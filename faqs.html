Frequently Asked Questions

Your questions answered about Qatar's smart escrow solution

Why should businesses choose Esqro over traditional escrow services? +

Esqro eliminates transaction risk and builds trust between business parties by acting as a neutral intermediary that holds funds securely until contract conditions are met. Unlike traditional escrow services, our platform offers automated execution through smart contracts, real-time transparency, faster processing times, and lower fees while maintaining full Sharia compliance. Businesses benefit from reduced counterparty risk, streamlined payment processes, digital contract management, and the assurance that funds are only released when agreed-upon milestones or conditions are fulfilled.

For example, if a buyer is purchasing products from a supplier, the buyer can be assured that payment will only be released once the order is completed and delivered according to specifications. Meanwhile, the supplier has the guarantee that they will be paid immediately upon fulfilling their obligations, without delays or payment disputes.

What advantages does blockchain technology offer compared to conventional escrow methods? +

Blockchain provides immutable transaction records, automated execution through smart contracts, and complete transparency that traditional escrow cannot match. The distributed ledger ensures all parties have real-time visibility into transaction status, creates an unchangeable audit trail, and reduces operational costs through automation. Additionally, smart contracts enable complex conditional logic and milestone-based payments that would be cumbersome and expensive to manage manually in traditional escrow systems.

How does Esqro ensure transparency while maintaining security on a private blockchain? +

The private blockchain maintains transparency through a secure client portal where all authorized parties can view real-time transaction status, contract milestones, and payment histories in an easy-to-understand dashboard. While the underlying blockchain infrastructure remains private for security and compliance reasons, clients receive detailed transaction logs, automated notifications for every contract action, and access to immutable records of all activities related to their agreements. This approach provides the transparency benefits of blockchain technology while maintaining the privacy and regulatory compliance required for B2B transactions in Qatar's business environment.

Can contract terms be modified after a smart contract has been created? +

No, once a smart contract is deployed and agreed upon by both parties, the terms cannot be changed due to the immutable nature of blockchain technology. This immutability is actually a key security feature that prevents unauthorized modifications and ensures all parties can trust that the original agreement will be executed exactly as specified. If changes are needed, parties must mutually agree to terminate the existing contract and create a new one with updated terms, ensuring complete transparency and consent from all involved parties.

How are disputes between parties resolved on the platform? +

In Phase 1, disputes are handled through our internal mediation process where both parties can present their case along with supporting documentation uploaded to the platform. Our team reviews the contract terms, evidence provided, and facilitates resolution between the parties. If mediation fails, the dispute can be escalated to Qatar's traditional legal system or arbitration as specified in the original contract. The smart contract remains locked during dispute resolution to protect both parties' interests.

In future phases, we plan to integrate automated dispute resolution mechanisms and third-party arbitrators directly into the platform for faster resolution. This will be possible by using custom built oracles and using APIs to connect with ERP tools that our clients use.

What security measures protect client funds from cyber threats and hacking attempts? +

Client funds are protected through multiple layers of security: all fiat currency is held in regulated Qatar bank accounts rather than on the blockchain, eliminating the risk of cryptocurrency exchange hacks. Our private blockchain will use enterprise-grade encryption and access controls. We aim to implement multi-signature authorization for fund transfers, regular security penetration testing, and maintain insurance coverage for operational risks during later phases of our product.

Additionally, our tokenization system ensures that even if the platform were compromised, attackers cannot directly access the underlying fiat currency held in traditional banking infrastructure, providing an extra layer of protection that pure cryptocurrency platforms cannot offer.

What licenses and regulatory approvals does Esqro need to operate legally? +

Under Article 18 of Qatar Financial Centre's Digital Asset Regulations 2024, our tokenization platform would require multiple licenses to operate legally:

Required QFC Licenses:

  • Validation License - To confirm ownership of rights and issue certificates of validation for assets being tokenized
  • Token Generation License - To create tokens on blockchain infrastructure representing underlying assets
  • Token Custody Services License - To hold and control tokens on behalf of clients, including managing private keys and transaction capabilities
  • Token Exchange License - To operate our trading platform that brings together buyers and sellers of tokenized assets
  • Token Transfer Services License - To facilitate the transfer of tokens between parties

Banking and Financial Services: Given that we hold client fiat currency in Qatar bank accounts, we would need appropriate banking partnerships and potentially additional financial services licenses depending on the structure of our custody arrangements.

Compliance Requirements: The regulations require licensed entities to have adequate systems to prevent conflicts of interest when providing multiple token services, and all operations must comply with the QFC's technology guidance for token infrastructure. We want to engage with QFC regulatory counsel to obtain definitive guidance on the complete licensing framework required for our specific business model.

How does the platform handle situations where a company cannot make full upfront payments? +

When Company A cannot pay the full amount upfront, our platform accommodates installment payments through an automated escrow notification system. Upon contract initiation, Company A receives notifications to deposit their scheduled payment amounts into the designated escrow account, which then triggers the generation of corresponding tokens that are sent to the smart contract. The system sends timely reminders for upcoming payment deadlines to ensure compliance with the agreed schedule.

Meanwhile, Company B receives real-time notifications about the smart contract's status, including confirmation of each payment received, tokens generated, and overall contract progress. This approach maintains transparency for both parties while allowing flexible payment terms, with the smart contract automatically updating to reflect the current funding status and releasing deliverables or milestones as payments are completed according to the predetermined schedule.

Is there a risk of tokens being permanently locked in escrow contracts? +

Yes, tokens can be indefinitely locked in escrow due to poorly written smart contracts or unforeseen technical issues. However, our platform implements a comprehensive fail-safe mechanism to prevent permanent token lockup. We have built-in administrative controls that allow us to move tokens from the smart contract to a secure admin account in exceptional circumstances where the contract cannot execute properly or parties cannot resolve disputes through normal channels.

This emergency intervention capability is designed as a last resort measure and will be used sparingly, with proper governance protocols and documentation for any such action. Our primary objective is to ensure smart contracts execute automatically as intended, but this fail-safe provides essential protection for users' assets while maintaining the integrity and security of the tokenization process.

Is Esqro a cryptocurrency platform, and how does it differ from traditional crypto services? +

No, our platform is not a cryptocurrency. While we utilize blockchain technology, there are fundamental differences between our tokenization platform and traditional cryptocurrencies. Cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum are digital currencies designed as alternatives to fiat money and primarily serve as mediums of exchange or stores of value. In contrast, our platform creates asset-backed tokens that represent ownership rights in real-world assets such as real estate, commodities, contracts, or business agreements.

Each token on our platform is tied to a specific underlying asset with intrinsic value, whereas cryptocurrencies typically derive value from market speculation and adoption. Our tokens are purpose-built for specific business transactions and contractual relationships, with predetermined terms and conditions embedded in smart contracts. The platform focuses on digitizing and automating traditional business processes rather than creating a new form of currency.

Additionally, our tokens are designed to comply with regulatory frameworks for asset tokenization and may have restricted transferability based on the underlying asset's nature, unlike cryptocurrencies which are generally designed for open, permissionless trading. Essentially, we're using blockchain as a tool to modernize asset management and business transactions, not to create an alternative monetary system.
